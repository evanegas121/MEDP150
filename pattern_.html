For the color rule pattern, I used complementary colors, or colors opposite on the color wheel, that I got from the Adobe Color CC website in which there were different shades of green and pink. I liked the idea of having garden-like colors and it was fun playing around to see the different palette of swatches. I ended up saving a few different sets of colors in my library. I really like the pastel analogous colors that I got but ended up using the complementary swatches. The green and pinks reminded me of nature, so I played around and created a flower like image inside a light pink filled circle. After creating the one element I played around with the pattern properties. I had it layed out as brick with an offset of ½. I also tried using hex by column which had the same result as the brick offset. I also lowered the height to have the rows closer together but didn't change the width as I didn’t want the circles to overlap. I choose to have a darker pink(maybe fuschia or magenta) as a solid background rather than having it see-through. I think it would be cute wrapping paper, some scrap paper for journaling or scrapbooking.
